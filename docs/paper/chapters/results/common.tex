\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

This section presents both our internal validation results and the official evaluation results for each phase across the four test batches of the 14th BioASQ challenge. Our systems are labeled \texttt{bioinfo-0} through \texttt{bioinfo-4}, representing different configurations submitted across batches.

\subsection{Internal Retrieval Testing}
\label{sec:internal-retrieval}

We conducted extensive internal testing of the various retrieval strategies introduced this year, including Context-1-based retrieval, HyDE query expansion, and different fusion approaches (RRF and weighted sum) across multiple parameter configurations. These experiments were designed to measure the marginal contribution of each retrieval component to end-to-end retrieval performance.

\subsection{Internal Validation: Reranker Models}
\label{sec:internal-validation}

Prior to the competition, we conducted internal validation of our reranker models using the 13B golden datasets (batches~1--4) as held-out evaluation sets. Performance was measured using the BioASQ-specific MAP metric (MAP@10, based on PubMed article-level relevance judgments). Table~\ref{tab:reranker-val} summarizes the validation results for the top-performing models.

\begin{table}[ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Internal validation results for selected reranker models on the 13B golden datasets. \texttt{map-bioasq@10} is the primary metric.}
  \label{tab:reranker-val}
  \begin{tabular}{lcc}
    \hline
    \textbf{Model} & \textbf{Configuration} & \textbf{MAP-bioasq@10} \\
    \hline
    LLaMA Nemotron rerank 1B & E2-S4, multi\_neg\_pairwise, InfoNCE & 0.9995 \\
    LLaMA Nemotron rerank 1B & E5, pairwise & 0.9970 \\

    BGE-reranker-base & E5, pairwise & 0.6686 \\
    nboost/pt-biobert-base-msmarco & E5, pairwise & 0.6608 \\
    ncbi/MedCPT-Cross-Encoder & E5, pairwise & 0.6404 \\

    \hline
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The LLaMA Nemotron reranker models achieved outstanding validation performance, substantially outperforming all BERT-based architectures. Among the smaller models, BGE-reranker-v2-m3 demonstrated the strongest performance, followed by the biomedical domain-specific models. The gap between general-domain and biomedical-specific rerankers was narrower than in previous years, suggesting that larger pretrained models can effectively transfer to biomedical text without extensive domain-specific pretraining.
